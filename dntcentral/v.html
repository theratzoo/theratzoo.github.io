About


What is "Magic the Gathering"?

For those who don't know, Magic the Gathering is a collectable card game that focuses on strategy, critical thinking, and other vital skills. The game is played with two people and decks, in a best of three format. Magic the Gathering has been around for 25 years, and has a variety of different ways to play the game, the best of course is Vintage...


About Vintage

Have a passion for winning on turn one? How about playing with the game's oldest cards? Want to play with a deck that costs more than an average car? If so, Vintage is the format for you! With every card available in the game, including all the over-powered ones, the possibilities are endless! From decks that use the best cards in Magic the Gathering to decks that souly beat those decks, there are decks that akin to anyone's playstyle. Below is a list of the format's many decks:


  • Jeskai Mentor
  • Paradoxical Storm
  • Ravager Shops
  • Dredge
  • Inferno Oath
  • Survival

If you think that Vintage is too intimidating for you (the cards are older than most people in this room), than try a newer format like Modern! More can be found about Modern from this amazing website.

Playing the Deck

The deck has many different lines of attack. For starters, the deck uses its namesake card, Paradoxical Outcome, to draw a lot of cards. Playing a plethora of zero mana artifacts that produce mana allow Paradoxical Outcome to not only draw several cards, but also net mana rather than cost mana. Returning the moxen and other cheap artifacts works well with the deck's primary win condition, Monastery Mentor. If the Paradoxical Outcome plan were to fail, the deck has two other combos to win. One of said combos is Voltaic Key plus Time Vault, which lets the deck take an arbitrarily large number of turns. The other combo, Tinker for Blightsteel Colossus, lets the deck win through artifact hate such as Null Rod or Stony Silence or outrace hate from Ravager Shops.


Notable Cards


Library of Alexandria

Library of Alexandria is the number one best card to play turn one in Vintage, assuming you did not mulligan. The land's ability will win the game against any fair blue deck (Jeskai Mentor, Landstill, Delver, BUG Leovold, etc.) assuming they lack a Wasteland or Strip Mine. Library of Alexandria can also help dig for answers like Force of Will against Ravager Shops or other decks with taxing effects. One note of the card: do not cast a card in hand before using the land's ability if you have 7 cards in hand.

Ancestral Recall

Ancestral Recall is perhaps the best card in Magic the Gathering. Approaching $3,000 USD, this one mana spell is well worth its monetary value- drawing three cards for one mana is value! While jamming the spell is tempting, it is often best to play cantrips like Preordain before casting the Ancestral Recall, as Mental Misstep is a common card in Vintage. Also, to avoid discarding, casting Ancestral Recall on the opponent's upkeep is the correct play.

Sensei's Divining Top

It remains a mystery why this card is not restricted in Vintage. Have you ever played against Sensei's Divining Top in Legacy? The game takes an eternity, as 90% of players have no idea what the heck they are doing when activating the ability. Sadly, I fall in that 90% category. The only advice I can give is to tap top to draw a card, then in response either Paradoxical Outcome or Voltaic Key the Sensei's Divining Top, and then tap said artifact again to draw that sweet new card. In terms of the other ability, good luck with that one. Maybe you'll learn how to use it properly after a slow play warning or two is issued...
