5 Color Humans


Matchup Notes:

Overall, the matchup is very close, usually dependent on draws and turn order. Both sides have both very poor cards and very powerful cards in the matchup. Your role is to play as the control deck, trying to prevent them from killing you before around turn 5 or 6. After that, grinding with an army of Blade Splicer golems or an Eldrazi Displacer can outrace their Mantis Riders and Thalia's Lieutenants. After sideboard, both decks get access to more removal and bomby threats, making the matchup still variable. Overall, our best chance at winning the game is by keeping our life total high, building up a board of defenders, and, upon controlling the game, closing it out with a flier or an alpha strike.

Our best cards:



Despite the speed of Humans, our best cards are our more mana-intensive cards. Of course, Path to Exile is valuable, as without it a turn two mantis rider on the play spells doom. In addition, good defenders like Restoration Angel and Blade Splicer are key, as they can help defend our life total. Fliers are important for us, as we can easily flood the board with ground creatures like Blade Splicer tokens and a bunch of chump blockers to stop their Champion of Pariahs and Thalia's Lieutenants, but struggle more with beating a mantis rider (especially one getting pumped by a noble hierarch or Thalia's Lieutenant). Our single best card in the deck, surprisingly, is Eldrazi Displacer. The card on its own is a 3 mana 3/3 (or 2 mana with Eldrazi Temple), which isn't exciting in the matchup. However, its ability to kill a Phantasmal Image, blink a Blade Splicer to make a token, or just stop their fliers from killing us makes it very versatile and valuable. Since humans lacks removal preboard (and only runs a couple postboard), there is no threat of Eldrazi Displacer dying, so it is not hard to get value off of it and use said value to snowball the advantage into a victory.

Our worst cards:



Not surprisingly, Thalia, Guardian of Thraben is one of our worst cards, as they run 0 relevant non creature spells pre board (Aether Vial doesn't really do anything for them after turn one). However, even Thalia, Guardian of Thraben can be valuable, as a 2/1 first striker is a good blocker vs many of their creatures. In fact, I tend to favor Thalia, Guardian of Thraben over Leonin Arbiter, as the latter lacks first-strike, and, because of their aether Aether Vials and low curve, ghost quartering them out of the game with Leonin Arbiter is not a realistic gameplan.

OP's best cards:



As mentioned previously, Mantis Rider is very bad for us, especially turn 2 (not too different from a turn two True-Name Nemesis in legacy). Reflector Mage is also very good vs. us, as the tempo it generates makes up for any value they lose from blinking a Flickerwisp or Restoration Angel. Thalia's Lieutenant can be annoying, especially one that accelerates their aggro plan. The deck's 1-of cards are also good vs us, like Restoration Angel, Dark Confidant, and Kessig Malcontents.

Sideboarding:



One the draw, typically trim on Leonin Arbiters and Thalia, Guardian of Thraben. Leonin Arbiter is often too slow on the draw, but on the play can steal games paired with Ghost Quarter. Therefore, I like to keep in more Leonin Arbiters on the play and instead trim on Thraben Inspectors. For cards to bring in, all removal and good creatures like Shalai, Voice of Plenty, Mirran Crusader, and even Auriok Champion are key. Worship is also an amazing card here, as they cannot beat it without a Reclamation Sage (or us not having sufficent creatures in play).

Important Interactions:



Due to Phantasmal Image's ability of sacrificing itself whenever its the target of a spell or ability, Flickerwisp and Eldrazi Displacer gain strength due to the ease of eliminating the Phantasmal Image. Also, as with other Aether Vial decks, Flickerwisp can reset the opponent's Aether Vial's counters, especially useful when you plan to constrain them of mana and out race them. Don't forget that you can still put into play creatures that were bounced by Reflector Mage the next turn, so long as you have an Aether Vial with enough counters active!
